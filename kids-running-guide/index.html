🌟 Why Run?

Running is one of the coolest things you can do with your body. It doesn't need fancy equipment, you can do it almost anywhere, and every time you run, you get a little bit stronger, a little bit faster, and a whole lot happier.

💡 Fun fact: Your body releases special chemicals called endorphins when you run — scientists call it the "runner's high." It's like your brain giving you a high-five! ✋

What running does for you:

  • Makes your heart stronger (it's a muscle — exercise it!)
  • Builds strong bones and muscles
  • Helps you sleep better at night
  • Boosts your mood and confidence
  • Gives you more energy during the day
  • Teaches you that practice makes progress

👟 Getting Started: Gear

You really only need one thing: a pair of comfortable trainers. That's the beauty of running.

Your Running Kit Checklist

  • Breathable t-shirt or vest
  • Shorts or leggings (whatever feels good)
  • Socks (no blisters please!)
  • Water bottle
  • Sunscreen & a cap (if it's sunny)
👟 Shoe tip: Your running shoes should have about a thumb's width of space at the front. Feet swell a little when you run! And replace them every 400–500 km — that's roughly Cape Town to Knysna and back.

🔥 Warming Up

Never skip your warmup! Cold muscles are like cold elastic bands — they can snap. Spend 5 minutes getting your body ready.

Dynamic Warmup (3 minutes each side)

  1. Leg swings — Hold onto a wall, swing one leg forward and back 10 times. Switch sides.
  2. Arm circles — Big circles forward 10 times, then backward 10 times.
  3. High knees (on the spot) — Jog in place, bringing knees up. 30 seconds.
  4. Butt kicks — Jog in place, kicking your heels toward your bum. 30 seconds.
  5. Walking lunges — Take big steps forward, bending both knees. 5 per leg.

🏃 How to Actually Run

There's no "perfect" way to run — everyone's body is different. But here are some tips that help most runners feel better:

Running Form Basics

  • Look ahead, not down — pick a spot about 10 metres in front of you
  • Relax your shoulders — don't hunch them up by your ears!
  • Arms swing front-to-back — not across your body
  • Hands like you're holding a crisp — gentle fist, not clenched tight
  • Land softly — aim for quiet feet. If you can hear yourself slapping the ground, try to land lighter
  • Breathe in through your nose, out through your mouth — or whatever feels comfortable
🎮 Imagine this: Pretend there's a string attached to the top of your head, gently pulling you upward. That's your posture! Tall and relaxed.

📈 Building Up Slowly

The biggest mistake new runners make? Doing too much, too soon. Your muscles and bones need time to adapt. The rule is: increase by no more than 10% per week.

4-Week Beginner Plan

1 Walk 1 min / Run 1 min × 6 Walk 1 min / Run 1 min × 6 Walk 1 min / Run 2 min × 5
2 Walk 1 min / Run 2 min × 5 Walk 1 min / Run 3 min × 4 Run 5 min / Walk 1 min × 3
3 Run 5 min / Walk 1 min × 3 Run 8 min / Walk 1 min × 2 Run 10 min straight! 🎉
4 Run 10 min / Walk 1 min × 2 Run 15 min straight! Run 20 min — you're a runner! 🏆

🎮 Make It Fun!

Running doesn't have to be all serious. Here are some games to play while you run:

Running Games

  • 🔴🟢 Red Light, Green Light — Someone shouts "green light" to run, "red light" to freeze. Try it with friends!
  • 🏷️ Tag variations — Freeze tag, tunnel tag (crawl through legs to unfreeze), shadow tag (step on someone's shadow)
  • 🗺️ Explore & Map — Pick a new route each time. Draw a map of where you ran afterward.
  • 🎵 Music Miles — Make a playlist of your favourite songs and run for the whole playlist.
  • 🦸 Superhero Sprints — Between two lamp posts, you're The Flash. Then walk to recover. Repeat!
  • 🔢 Counting Game — Count how many dogs, blue cars, or red doors you see on your run.

💧 Staying Safe & Hydrated

Safety Rules 🛡️

  • Always tell an adult where you're going and when you'll be back
  • Run with a buddy or in a safe area where adults are around
  • Face traffic if you're on a road — so you can see cars coming
  • Wear bright colours so drivers can see you
  • Stop if something hurts — pain is your body's way of saying "slow down"
  • Drink water before, during (if it's hot), and after your run
💧 Hydration rule: Drink when you're thirsty. If your pee is dark yellow, you need more water. Light yellow = you're good to go!

💡 Fun Running Facts

🐆 Fastest animal on land?
The cheetah can run at 112 km/h — that's faster than most cars are allowed to drive! A cheetah would finish a 100m race in about 3 seconds.
🦴 How many bones in your foot?
Each foot has 26 bones, 33 joints, and over 100 muscles and tendons. Your feet are engineering marvels — treat them well!
🌍 Longest race in the world?
The Self-Transcendence 3100 Mile Race in New York takes runners 52 days to finish. They run about 95 km every single day. That's more than two marathons daily!
👶 Youngest marathon runner?
Budhia Singh of India ran 48 marathons before the age of 5. (Don't try this — his story is controversial, and kids your age should focus on fun, short runs!)

🏁 Your First 5K

Once you can run for 20 minutes without stopping, you're ready to think about your first 5K! That's 5 kilometres — about 12 laps of a standard running track.

5K Timeline

  • Weeks 1–4: Follow the beginner plan above (run/walk intervals)
  • Weeks 5–8: Run without stopping, adding 1–2 minutes each week
  • Week 8+: Run 30 minutes continuously = roughly a 5K!
  • Goal: Your first parkrun (free, timed 5K every Saturday morning) 🎉
🏅 Did you know? parkrun is a free, weekly 5K event held in parks all over South Africa and the world. You can walk, jog, or run. There's no time limit, and it's super welcoming to kids. Find one near you at parkrun.co.za.

✅ Quick-Start Summary

  1. Put on comfortable shoes 👟
  2. Warm up for 5 minutes 🔥
  3. Start with walk/run intervals (like 1 min run, 1 min walk) ⏱️
  4. Run 3 times a week with rest days in between 📅
  5. Stretch after each run (use the timer above!) 🧘
  6. Drink water 💧
  7. Have fun! That's the most important rule 🎉
